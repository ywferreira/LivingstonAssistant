# Make a Family Emergency Plan

## Guidelines: Planning for an Emergency

These tips provide a place to start to get your family ready before an emergency or disaster occurs.

* [Be informed](http://www.ready.gov/be-informed). Learn to recognize signs of potential impending emergency situations. Know where to tune in for emergency information. Make sure you’re signed up for local Emergency Alerts.
  + [Register for Livingston’s Emergency & Police Alerts](/610/Emergency-Police-Alerts)
* [Build a kit](http://www.ready.gov/build-a-kit). That way, you’ll be ready if an emergency occurs. View a [Kit Supply List (PDF)](/DocumentCenter/View/1999).
* [A family emergency plan](https://www.ready.gov/make-a-plan) will help ensure that you have critical contact information.
  + View family emergency plan sheets [for parents (PDF)](/DocumentCenter/View/2002) and [for kids (PDF)](/DocumentCenter/View/2001).
* [Get involved](http://www.ready.gov/get-involved). Whether you want to volunteer to help others or end up needing help yourself, know where to go and whom to call.
* Get your [kids involved](http://www.ready.gov/kids).
