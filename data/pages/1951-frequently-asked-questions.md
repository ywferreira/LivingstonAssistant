# Frequently Asked Questions

[UPDATED: What area of the Livingston Mall site is included?](#)
:   The property included in the Community Visioning sessions is made up of 42.6 acres located between Eisenhower Parkway, Walnut Street and Route 510 (South Orange Avenue). The 15.8 acres located on the site of the former Sears store is now subject to a Redevelopment Plan that authorizes 376 units of housing — specifically, one- and two-bedroom apartments with a maximum of 81 townhouses.

    The Redevelopment Plan for the Sears site was recently adopted as part of a settlement with the Fair Share Housing Center. As of today, no site plan application for the Sears site has been filed, and no one knows when a site plan application will be submitted.

    Although a Redevelopment Plan exists for the Sears site, the Community Visioning sessions may result in ideas from community members for the overall Livingston Mall site. The eventual redeveloper of the Sears site may ultimately work together with the Township, the project team, and other redevelopers at the Livingston Mall site and incorporate feedback from the Community Visioning sessions into their overall site plan.

    View [Livingston Mall Updates (PDF)](/DocumentCenter/View/29001) and the [Livingston Mall Redevelopment Map (PDF)](/DocumentCenter/View/29002).

[What are Community Visioning sessions?](#)
:   Community Visioning sessions are basically open meetings that allow all members of the community to come together and share their thoughts, ideas, and visions for the Livingston Mall site. These sessions are orchestrated through an intense brainstorming process over a short period of time that includes multiple feedback loops.

[What is the purpose of this planning effort?](#)
:   The 6-day Community Visioning Session project will produce a proposed Conceptual Vision Plan for the future redevelopment of the Livingston Mall site, including building form and function, and modes of transportation. The Conceptual Vision Plan will be used to create a Redevelopment Plan for the site.

[What can I expect from the Community Visioning Sessions?](#)
:   The Community Visioning sessions employ a collaborative, intensive, evolving approach to improve design outcomes through continuous community review and feedback. This means engaging as many members of the community as possible to provide information and input to allow for development of appropriate architectural and design solutions.

[Why is the Township using this Community Visioning Session approach?](#)
:   Community Visioning Sessions provide several benefits to address the complexities of this unique property, including:

    * Bringing the community together into one room to create comprehensive, thoughtful, and prompt solutions to complex problems in what could otherwise be a drawn-out, opaque process.
    * Employing a collaborative and evolving approach based on continuous community review and feedback, which leads to better design outcomes.
    * Providing a path forward for the Livingston Mall site that not only responds to and incorporates the community’s vision for the property, but is also realistic and achievable.
    * Helping participants to understand the relationship between community input and design decisions and giving contributors a well-earned sense of ownership over the Conceptual Vision Plan.

[What happens at the Hands-On Visioning Session?](#)
:   The session will begin with a presentation, during which participants will learn about the project, market realities, the ground rules of the visioning sessions, and the principles for creating great places in communities.

    Working in small groups, participants will identify issues, opportunities, and constraints. Once that is completed, they will draw their ideas onto an aerial photo of the site using pens and colored markers. Members of the Visioning Team will help facilitate the discussion. At the end of the session, each group will share their vision with all of those in attendance.

[What happens at the Pin-Up Reviews?](#)
:   Following the Hands-On Vision Session, the Visioning Team will begin brainstorming ideas and working on concepts based on all of the input received up to that point.

    On Tuesday evening, the team will pin up their initial ideas onto a large presentation board for review and feedback by attendees. These ideas will frame the Conceptual Vision Plan.

    On Wednesday evening, the team will host another pin-up review of initial design concepts for additional feedback. The purpose of both of these sessions is to share the evolution of ideas and gather additional input on the designs.

[What happens at the Work-in-Progress Presentation?](#)
:   Saturday’s Work-in-Progress presentation will detail the working version of the Conceptual Vision Plan. The presentation will include an illustrative master plan and three-dimensional and digital images illustrating the potential for the future of the mall site.

    The goal of the Work-in-Progress presentation is to ensure that the concept in progress effectively reflects the vision crafted over the week’s community sessions.

    Community members are encouraged to provide feedback before the Visioning Team further expands on the Conceptual Vision Plan.

[What happens next?](#)
:   After completing all of the Community Visioning Sessions, the project team will continue to develop the Conceptual Vision Plan, ultimately creating a product that will provide the basis for a site-wide redevelopment plan. This plan will provide a detailed site layout, including features like road layouts, building locations and uses, and guiding zoning and design specifications.

    The Conceptual Vision Plan, as well as a summary of the Community Visioning process, will be presented to the Township Council at a subsequent public meeting.

Community members will be able to drop in to check on progress, ask questions, and share their ideas and input.
