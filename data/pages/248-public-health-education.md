# Public Health / Education

## About

The Nursing/Health Education Division of the Livingston Health Department focuses on providing excellence in health-related services for our residents by educating, promoting, and responding to community health issues. We strive to promote the best practices by fostering, providing, and increasing awareness of optimal health and wellness initiatives for our community. To accomplish this goal, we offer various health screenings, health education, illness prevention services, and other programs for our residents. We maintain close collaboration and partner with local physicians, community organizations, and agencies to provide and promote our outstanding health services.

## Zufall Health Centers

Zufall Health Centers offers health services to those in need regardless of their insurance and/or ability to pay. Zufall accepts a variety of insurance policies but also offers a sliding fee discount for services. Zufall offers a variety of health services such as cancer screenings, vaccinations, dental health services, pediatrics, and more. For more information about Zufall Health Centers services and locations, visit [Zufall Health](https://www.zufallhealth.org/).

## FIFA World Cup Games and Events

The New Jersey Department of Health has shared resources for anyone that is planning to attend any FIFA World Cup games or other events within the area of the games. The resources include where to find medical care, health and safety information, and the match schedule.

Anyone who is attending the FIFA World Cup games or events may also download the Everbridge Public Safety App to receive transportation updates, safety alerts, and game information.

To access this information, visit [The New Jersey Department of Health World Cup NJ.](https://www.nj.gov/health/world-cup/)

## Alert: Air Quality

The air quality may be impacted at times during the summer months due to air pollution/wildfire events, extreme heat, and high ozone days. Certain people such as those with COPD, asthma, heart disease, lung diseases, or are pregnant may be at higher risk of health effects on days when the air quality is poor. Anyone with a condition that may be affected by poor air quality should have a plan in place for days when the air quality may be impacted.

To check the air quality, visit [AirNow.gov](https://www.airnow.gov/?city=Livingston&state=NJ&country=USA).

On poor air quality days, those at risk for health effects should: ensure they have their medications and take them as prescribed/needed, stay indoors in air conditioning with the windows and doors closed, and avoid outdoor activities and consider wearing a mask (N-95) if going outdoors.

## Alert: Infant Formula Recalled

Nara Organics is recalling lots of their powered infant formula due to possible contamination of Clostridium botulinum. Botulism is a rare and potentially fatal illness. Infants that are affected by botulism may experience difficulty feeding, constipation, loss of head control, and weak facial expression.

For more information regarding the recall, visit the [US Food and Drug Administration: Nara Organics Recall](https://www.fda.gov/safety/recalls-market-withdrawals-safety-alerts/nara-organics-recalls-all-lots-nara-infant-formula-because-possible-health-risk?permalink=A2536D05343A8320178C3D4696236381ED1BC45DFC32A046BC9F0366DBE49345).

## H5N1 Bird Flu

H5N1 is an influenza A virus that wild birds and waterfowl can carry and spread to other animals and humans. Infected wild birds and waterfowl may show no signs of illness but are still able to spread the disease. At this time, the risk of H5N1 Bird Flu is low for humans. To learn more about H5N1 Bird Flu, visit the [NJ Department of Health](https://nj.gov/H5N1/).

If you find any sick or dead wild birds, do not handle them. Report any sick or dead wild birds online to the [New Jersey DEP Fish and Wildlife.](https://survey123.arcgis.com/share/749040214c974bb88c89c58483a51ab8) For any sick or dead poultry you may, do not handle them and contact the NJ Department of Agriculture's Division of Animal Health at phone number 609-671-6400.
