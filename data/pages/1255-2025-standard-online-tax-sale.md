# 2025 Standard Online Tax Sale

In New Jersey, all municipalities are required to hold at least one tax sale per year. The properties on which liens are sold at the tax sale are those with delinquent property taxes and/or municipal charges. (Pursuant to N.J.S.A 54:5-19). The Township of Livingston will hold its annual online standard tax lien sale on **Thurs****day****,****October 30, 2025 at 8:30 a.m.**

[View the 2025 Tax Sale Public Notice (PDF).](/DocumentCenter/View/33425)

All delinquencies for the 2024 tax year are now subject to the tax-sale process.

Visit [Livingston's Official Tax Lien Auction Site](https://livingston.newjerseytaxsale.com/) for a listing and registration.

Per this process, delinquencies will be subject to a 2% cost of tax sale (up to $100). In addition, delinquencies will be subject to a mailing fee of $25 for each mailing. Two mailings are scheduled during this process.

After September 11, 2025, the amount owed is calculated as follows:

Delinquent Taxes/Other Municipal Charges   +   Interest   +   Cost of Sale   +   Mailing Fees   =

Total Amount Owed

To receive the exact amount owed, please contact the Tax Office at 973-992-5000 ext. #5121, #5122, #5123, or #5124, or [email the Tax Department](mailto:tax@livingstonnj.org).

**All payments received after September 11, 2025, MUST be made using guaranteed funds:**

* cash,
* cashier’s check or
* money order.

Any other forms of payment **WILL NOT** be accepted. All other checks – attorney, business, lender, and personal – **WILL****be returned**. In addition, the credit/debit card payment options **WILL NOT** be available after September 11, 2025.

The statute also requires a Public Notice of Tax Sale. If payment is not received by **September 26, 2025**, the property owner’s name will appear in this Public Notice.

**Note: Failure of the property owner to receive a notice of tax sale properly mailed by the Tax Collector shall not constitute grounds to void the subsequent tax sale.**
