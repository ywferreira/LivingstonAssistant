# New Jersey’s Lead-Based Paint Law

Livingston Township is reminding landlords of their responsibilities under New Jersey’s Lead Safe Certification Law, which requires certain **rental** properties to undergo a visual inspection for lead hazards. **This is for rental properties only.**

## Key Deadlines

* March 20, 2026 – All inspections must at least be scheduled if they have not yet been completed.

Livingston Township has partnered with LEW Environmental Services to help property owners meet these requirements, though property owners may choose to use the company of their choice. Landlords should contact LEW Environmental directly, or another environmental service company, to schedule their inspection and prepare the necessary documentation.

Check if your property requires a lead-based paint inspection here: LEW Environmental – [Livingston Lead Inspections](http://www.lewenvironmental.com/livingston)

## Why Are Lead-Safe Inspections Required?

Lead exposure can have severe health consequences, especially for children. The law was updated in 2022 to extend inspection requirements to one- and two-unit **rental** properties, in addition to multi-unit dwellings that were already regulated.

The goal is to prevent childhood lead poisoning. Supporting this, the CDC has:

* Updated guidelines requiring blood lead level testing for children at ages 1 and 2.
* Lowered the threshold for what is considered an “elevated” blood lead level.

## Which Dwellings Are Exempt?

All single-family, two-family, and multiple **rental** dwellings must be inspected unless they meet one of the following exemptions:

* Properties built in 1978 or later.
* Seasonal rentals (single- or two-family dwellings rented for less than six months per year, with no consecutive lease renewals).
* Dwellings certified to be free of lead-based paint under N.J.A.C. 5:17-3.16(b).
* Multiple dwellings registered with the New Jersey Department of Community Affairs (DCA) for at least ten years with no outstanding lead violations under the “Hotel and Multiple Dwelling Law” (N.J.S.A. 55:13A-1).
* Multiple dwellings with a valid DCA certificate of inspection showing no paint violations.
* Properties with a valid lead-safe certificate issued under P.L.2021, c.182 (valid for two years from issuance).
