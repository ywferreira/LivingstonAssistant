# Engineering

## About

Welcome to the Township of Livingston Engineering Department. We are committed to improving the quality of life for all of our residents through diligently overseeing the operation, maintenance, and improvement of the Township’s infrastructure in a cost-effective and environmentally-friendly manner. We work to provide high-quality and efficient engineering, planning, and construction management services to the Township while providing related services for other Township departments, residents, businesses, and visitors.

We strive to provide good customer service, to continuously improve the services that we provide, and to fully support the overall goals of the Township.

## Services

The Engineering Department is responsible for overseeing construction activities within the Township rights-of-way to ensure the safety of the public. This includes constructing, reconstructing, and maintaining Township facilities, including roads, utilities (water, storm, and sanitary sewer), parks, buildings, and any other Township infrastructure. Our services include:

* Designing infrastructure improvements by preparing plans and specifications for public bidding in accordance with all local, state, and federal standards. Funding for these projects is secured through capital funds and grants.
* Issuing road opening permits and inspections to ensure compliance with local standards.
* Reviewing applications submitted for approval to the Livingston Planning Board and Zoning Board of Adjustment.
* Responding to requests from the public on a variety of requests and issuing information on flood zones, permitting, and other engineering documents.
* Ensuring environmental compliance on all improvements to local, state, and federal requirements. This includes reviewing all residential and commercial development applications for compliance with floodplain, wetland, steep slope, and riparian buffer requirements.
* Establishing engineering standards for all improvements and maintaining the Townships tax maps and Geographic Information System.
* Supervising and providing support services to the Department of Public Works, Water Department, Sewer Department, and Water Pollution Control Facility.
* Reviewing and inspecting applications for Lot Surface Drainage and Sump Pump ordinance compliance to ensure that adjoining properties are protected from runoff associated with new development.
* Issuing soil removal permits and inspections to ensure compliance with local standards.
* Ensuring compliance with state and federal Stormwater Management requirements.
* Performing searches for public information in response to OPRA requests.
