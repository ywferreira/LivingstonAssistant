# Utility Service Line Ownership

## Water Service Line Ownership

The water service line is the underground pipe which connects a building to the water main in the street (or easement). Please note that the water service shutoff box, also know as a curb box, is the responsibility of the Township of Livingston Water Department. Only Township of Livingston staff may operate this shutoff. If the box lid becomes missing, buried, or shifted, the Township will excavate or repair the box as necessary.

## Water Service Line Leaks

It is the property owner's responsibility to maintain the water service line past the water service shutoff.  This includes the point where the water service connects to the water service shutoff.  If a leak is found on the property owner's side of the service, the property owner will be notified and given 30 days to make the repair. If the repair is not completed within the 30 days, the water service will be turned off until the repairs are completed.

## Sewer Lateral Line Ownership

## The sewer lateral line is the underground pipe which connects a building to the sewer main in the street (or easement).  The sewer main is a larger diameter pipe located in the Township right-of-way in the roadway or an easement.  In general, the property owner is responsible for maintaining the sanitary sewer lateral and sanitary sewer cleanout, including replacing the lid, uncovering it if buried, and repairing it if broken.

## Sewer Lateral Line Backups

## It is the property owner's responsibility to keep the sewer lateral line clear, and if there is a backup they have to clear the sewer lateral line. This includes the entire length of the sewer lateral line from the house to the sewer main, regardless of if a portion of the sewer lateral line is in the street.  Certain types of sewer lateral line obstructions such as tree roots may require more aggressive clearing techniques such as jetting or root cutters to clear.  These clearing techniques may require more specialized technicians than a regular plumber.   It is the Township's responsibility to clear a backup in the sewer main in the street. If it is determined that there is a problem with the sewer lateral line itself, such as a pipe failure, which requires excavation to repair and that problem behind the curb, it is the property owner's responsibility to make these repairs. If the entirety of the sewer lateral is behind the curb going to an easement, then no portion of the sewer lateral is the Township's responsibility.  If there is a problem with the sewer lateral in the street then it is the Township's responsibility to make these repairs.
