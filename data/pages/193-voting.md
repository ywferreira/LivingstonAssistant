# Elections

## Voter Registration

Voters must register at least 21 days before an Election date.

The 2026 Primary Election is scheduled for Tuesday, June 2, 2026. The last day to register to vote for this election is May 12, 2026. Please call the Clerk's office for more information on where to vote or go to [Vote.NJ.Gov](https://www.nj.gov/state/elections/vote.shtml).

A New Jersey resident who is at least 17 years of age may pre-register to vote, but cannot vote until reaching the age of 18. [Register to vote](https://www.nj.gov/state/elections/voter-registration.shtml).

The Voter Registration Application can be submitted to make the following changes:

* New Registration
* Name Change
* Political Party Affiliation: An affiliation change must be made at least 55 days prior to Election\*
* Address Change
* Signature Update
* Vote By Mail

\*Another way to make an affiliation change is to complete the [Political Party Affiliation Declaration Form (PDF)](https://livingstonnj.org/DocumentCenter/View/25131/NJ-Political-Party-Affiliation-Declaration-Form). The declaration must be filed no later than 55 days preceding the primary in which the voter wishes to vote.

# Three Ways to Vote!

## 1. Early In-Person Voting:

Early in-person voting will only be offered at [specific locations](https://essexboardofelections.com/) in Essex County. Livingston residents may go to any of the locations offered in the County for early in-person voting.

## 2. In-Person Voting on Election Day, 6:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m.:

Your sample ballot has all the information you need -- bring it with you to your polling location on Election Day.

Livingston has 3 Polling Locations:

1. Livingston Public Library – 10 Robert H. Harp Drive, Meeting Room (Districts 1, 6, 7, 13, 20, 23, 24)
2. Livingston Senior/Community Center – 204 Hillside Avenue, Lower Level entrance (Districts 2, 3, 4, 5, 10, 11, 12, 15, 16, 19, 22, 25, 26)
3. Livingston Town Hall – 357 S. Livingston Avenue, Main Lobby (Districts 8, 9, 14, 17, 18, 21)

View [Voting Locations by District Number (PDF)](https://livingstonnj.org/DocumentCenter/View/25276/Polling-Locations-by-District).

Find your polling place on your **Sample Ballot** or use [New Jersey's Polling Place Search Tool](https://voter.svrs.nj.gov/polling-place-search).

Voting Machines:

All Essex County voting locations have new voting machines. Voting in Essex County is now officially done on paper ballots, whether mailed in or at a polling location.

[New Voting Machines and How to Vote in Essex County (videos)](https://essexboardofelections.com/training-videos/).

Poll workers are not allowed to accept mail-in ballots.

## 3. Vote By Mail:

You may opt in to vote by mail for any one or all future Elections by completing a [vote by mail ballot application (PDF)](https://www.essexclerk.com/_Content/pdf/forms/vote-mail-ballot-essex-english.pdf) or alternately, by visiting [NJ.Vote.Gov](https://www.nj.gov/state/elections/vote.shtml), selecting "Request A Ballot", and proceeding with Vote By Mail application.

Am I Registered to Vote by Mail?

Find out if you're an active "Vote by Mail" voter at the [New Jersey Voter Information Portal](https://vote.nj.gov/) under [Am I Registered?](https://voter.svrs.nj.gov/registration-check)

Opt-Out of Vote by Mail - You may opt-out of vote by mail at any time by completing [the opt-out form (PDF)](https://www.essexclerk.com/_Content/pdf/Opt-out-form-2022.pdf) and submitting it to Essex County.

Returning Your VBM Ballot - VBM Ballots can be mailed at the Post Office, placed in a standard mailbox, delivered to the [Essex County Board of Elections](https://essexboardofelections.com/vote-by-mail/) Office, or placed in an Essex County Mail-In Ballot Drop-Off Box. See information below for each option.

Vote by mail ballots CANNOT be returned to your polling place.
