# Zoning

## About

The employees in the Livingston Zoning Department administer the town’s zoning ordinances in order to promote the growth and development of the community. We are responsible for administering the town's subdivision and site plan ordinances, as well as tree removal. Our staff is readily available to help answer any questions from residents, in addition to assisting them in completing any necessary forms.

## Services

* Enforce zoning subdivisions
* Enact site plan ordinances
* Handle tree removal permits
* Handle commercial zoning permits

## Fees

Zoning fees are as follows:

* Residential Zoning (Accessory Structures\*):  $75
* Residential Zoning (All Other):  $140
* Commercial Signs:  $140
* Commercial Tenant:  $140
* Zoning Confirmation Letter:  $75

\*Accessory Structures: Deck, Fence, Retaining Wall, Portico, Front Porch, Gazebo/Pergola, and Raised Patio

## Zoning Permits

A [Zoning](/266/Zoning-Board) Permit is required for all fences, decks, raised patios, pools, hot tubs, spas, retaining walls, new homes and remodeling of homes, building additions, and sheds, regardless of their height and/or size. The residential Zoning Permit fee is $75 for sheds, fences, decks, retaining walls, porticos, front porches, gazebos/pergolas, and raised patios; the fee for all other residential work is $140.

## Commercial Zoning Permits

A Commercial Zoning Permit also applies to any business that wishes to change ownership, expand their operation, and change tenants or signs. The fee for a non-residential Zoning Permit is $140. Sign Permits are required for all freestanding signs, wall signs, and banner-type signs prior to their installation. If either the resident or business does not conform to the zoning in their particular area, and the Zoning Permit is denied, they have the right to apply to the Zoning Board for approval.

## Land Use Ordinance

Residence-based businesses or home offices are permitted and are subject to meet the standards in the [Township’s Land Use Ordinance](/590/Ordinances). These standards are intended to ensure that the home office does not negatively impact adjoining residents and the residential character of the neighborhood. You can obtain more information by reading [Livingston residence-based businesses or home offices (PDF)](/DocumentCenter/View/100).

To purchase a copy of the Land Use booklet, please contact the Township Clerk’s office at 973-992-5000 ext. #5400.
