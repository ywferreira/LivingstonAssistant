# Planning Board

## About Us

The Livingston Planning Board works to understand and enforce the Township’s Master Plan as well as monitor capital improvements, conditional use, and the applications for new developments. Each meeting of the Planning Board will ordinarily be preceded by a Conference Meeting. We welcome all members of the public to our meetings, held at Livingston Town Hall.

## Services:

* Review applications for development in Livingston
* Maintain and revise the Master Plan
* Monitor permitted activities and developments

## Regular Meetings

Meetings of the Board shall be held at Livingston Town Hall, 357 South Livingston Avenue, Livingston, New Jersey. Unless the Board determines otherwise, any Conference Meeting or Workshop Meeting shall start at 7:30 p.m., and any Regular Meeting or Special Meeting shall start thereafter.

Each Regular Meeting will ordinarily be preceded by a Conference Meeting.

Formal Action may be taken at any Meeting

Meetings are open to the Public in accordance with the Open Public Meetings Act.

## Important Notice: Planning and Zoning Board Agendas

Due to a recent scam in which individuals exploited information taken from past Planning and Zoning Board agendas, those older agendas have been removed from the Township website to protect sensitive data.

All future agendas will continue to be posted 48 hours before the meeting as usual; however, they will no longer include identifying links or personal information.

If you need access to a past agenda, please contact the Planning Board directly at [planning@livingstonnj.org](mailto:planning@livingstonnj.org) for assistance.

Thank you for your understanding as we take steps to ensure the security and integrity of Township information.

## Responsibilities

The nine (9) member board with two (2) alternates primary responsibilities include: development and adoption of Livingston’s Master Plan, review of land use applications in accordance with state and local regulations, and recommendations of revisions to land use ordinances to the governing body.

## Commercial/Site Plan Applicants

All commercial/site plan applicants are encouraged to meet with the Technical Review Advisory Committee (TRAC) prior to being scheduled for a public meeting. The Planning Administrator will determine if an application requires a TRAC review.
