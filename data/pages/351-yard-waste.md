# Yard Waste

## Yard Waste: What it is & How to Handle it

Yard waste refers to the following items:

* **Branches, Tree Limbs, and Brush**
  + How to handle these: Cut to four feet maximum length and 50 pounds maximum weight and tie in easily handled bundles. Bundles should not contain any difficult-to-handle sections that might endanger either the equipment or the workers. These should be cut down further into more manageable pieces.
    - NOTE: Logs or stumps will **not**be accepted.
* **Grass Clippings**
  + How to handle these: Bag grass clippings securely in black contractor bags weighing no more than 50 pounds. Leave the bags at the curb according to the Yard Waste Pickup Frequency guide below.
* **Leaves**
  + How to handle these: From January through September, bag leaves securely in black contractor bags weighing no more than 50 pounds and leave the bags at the curb. NOTE: No leaves will be picked up with yard waste during the period of the [Township's Leaf Collection Program](https://livingstonnj.org/385/Leaf-Collection).

## Yard Waste Pickup Frequency

Livingston's garbage collection vendor Basso Inc. provides yard waste collection as follows:

* Twice-per-week collection: January through December
  + **No leaves will be picked up with yard waste during the period of the**[Township's Leaf Collection Program](https://livingstonnj.org/385/Leaf-Collection)

**Remember:**During the Township's [Fall Leaf Collection Program](https://www.livingstonnj.org/385/Leaf-Collection) from late October through early December, leaves**must** be put on the curbline as directed on the [Leaf Collection Program](https://www.livingstonnj.org/385/Leaf-Collection) page, and will be picked up separately by the Township's Department of Public Works. Yard waste other than leaves will be collected by Basso Inc. twice a week.
