# Apply for a Marriage/Civil Union/Domestic Partnership Certificate

To apply for a Marriage/Civil Union/Partnership License, please contact the office at 973-535-7961 ext. #7105 to schedule an appointment.

Read the complete details on [How to Apply for a Marriage/Civil Union License (PDF).](https://livingstonnj.org/DocumentCenter/View/436/Application-to-Apply-for-MarriageCivil-Union-License-PDF)

* **If the applicants live in New Jersey:** The license must be obtained from the municipality in which either party resides.
* **If both parties live outside of New Jersey:** The license must be obtained in the municipality in which the ceremony takes place.

**NOTE**: A license applied for and issued in New Jersey may never be used outside of New Jersey, and a license issued in another state may never be used in New Jersey.

## Cost

Marriage/Civil Union/Domestic Partnership Certificate:   $28

## Documents Needed

[Complete the Application to Apply for a Marriage/Civil Union License (PDF).](/DocumentCenter/View/436/Application-to-Apply-for-MarriageCivil-Union-License-PDF)

Each applicant should bring the following documents to their appointment for a Marriage/Civil Union/Domestic Partnership Certificate:

* Birth Certificate (translated in English) or valid passport
  + Proof of address in the form of a valid driver’s license, current utility bill, lease, etc.
  + If marrying in Livingston and reside out of state, only a passport is required for residency proof
* Social security number is required; proof is requested but not required
* $28.00 cash to apply
* Officiant contact information: title, phone number, address
* One witness, 18 years or older, with a valid photo state ID
* If one or both of the applicants are divorced, a copy of the final decree(s) is requested but not required
* If one or both of the applicants have had a previous Civil Union dissolved, Domestic Partnership terminated or have had a Civil Union annulled, the decree(s) or the civil annulment documents is requested but not required
* If former spouse/Civil Union or domestic partner is deceased, the death certificate is requested but not required
* If one or more parties do not speak English, a translator is required who is not related
