## New Construction — Demolitions/New Utility Connections/Construction Details

Please refer to the [Water/Sewer Utility Connection Checklist (PDF)](/DocumentCenter/View/15826/Water-Sewer-disconnect-connections-checklist-PDF) for a step-by-step outline of the demolition and new utility connection process.

### Demolitions

For demolitions, the property owner is responsible for cutting and capping the existing water and sewer services prior to receiving a Demolition Permit from the Building Department. View the [Demolition Checklist (PDF)](https://www.livingstonnj.org/DocumentCenter/View/26000/Demolition-Checklist-PDF). The water service must be turned off and the meter removed prior to cutting and capping the lines. Any inspection requests must be made a minimum of 48 hours prior to the work. Valid and up-to-date utility markouts called in by the excavating party are required prior to excavation. [Demolition Preparation Application](/FormCenter/Water-Department-18/DEMO-preparation-95)

### Utility Connection Permit Application

The property owner is also responsible for any new water and sewer connections, up to, and including, the connection at the main. When a new utility connection is required, the [Utility Connection Permit Application](https://livingstonnj.org/FormCenter/Water-Department-18/Utility-Connection-Permit-Request-Form-85) must be completed. Upon successful completion, the Water and Sewer Department will review the form and respond back if the request has been approved or not. Before this application can be made, a utility markout, road opening permit, traffic control plan, and neighboring property disturbance notification as outlined below will be required.

## Proposed New Water/Sewer Service Line Size

Per Township Code §316-17 when the plumbing code requires a change in the size of a water service, the property owner is responsible for upgrading the service lateral from the meter up to, and including, the water main wet tap and abandoning the old service at the corporation. Please note 1.25" services are not allowed between the water main and the water shutoff. Whenever a 1.25" service is required, it shall be run as a 1.5" service and supplied with 1.5" water meter. The water service sizes for single family homes are outlined below.

* Less than 3.5 Bathrooms — Not Required
* Between 3.5 and 6 Bathrooms — 1-inch Water Service Line Upgrade Required
* Between 6.5 and 8 Bathrooms — 1.5-inch Water Service Line Upgrade Required
* More than 8 Bathrooms — 2-inch Water Service Line Upgrade Required

The sewer service line size for single-family homes is typically 4-inches or 6-inches in diameter. For Major Commercial or Multi-Family Residential Developments, the water & sewer line size shall be determined by the approved construction plans.

## Road Opening Permit

Sometimes new utility connections require a road opening permit. Depending on the road, a road Opening Permit from the Livingston Engineering Department, Essex County Department of Public Works, or NJ Division of Transportation will be required. For Township roads, view the [Application for Road Opening Permit (PDF)](https://livingstonnj.org/DocumentCenter/View/872/Road-Opening-Permit-and-Site-Safety-Application-PDF?bidId=). For county roads, visit the [Essex County Department of Public Works](https://www.ecdpw.org/permits_application.php). For state roads, view the [NJDOT Application for Utility Opening (PDF)](https://www.nj.gov/transportation/eng/forms/docs/maintenance/mt17a.pdf). A copy of the approved road opening permit must be included as part of the utility connection permit.

## Neighboring Property Disturbance Notification

A [Neighboring Property Disturbance Notification template](/DocumentCenter/View/30688/Water-Form-Letter---ROW-Excavation-on-Adjacent-Property) is available to download and customize to fit your needs. Sometimes new utility connections require excavation in the right-of-way (ROW) of an adjacent property to access the water or sewer main. This may disturb the driveway, sidewalk, grass, or landscaping on the neighboring property. The neighboring property must be notified by certified mail and any disturbances must be fully restored by the property owner of the new construction. A copy of the certified letter mailing receipt must be included as part of the utility connection permit.

## Traffic Control & Traffic Site Safety

The Livingston Police Department must be consulted and will determine if police officers are required during the new utility connection construction. A traffic control plan must be supplied to the Police Department for review. Written approval from the Police Department must be included as part of the utility connection permit. Any outside contractor working in the road or right of way agrees to adhere to [Article XI of the Township Code “Traffic Controls for Construction and Maintenance of Operations”.](/DocumentCenter/View/28735/Article-XI---Traffic-Controls-for-Construction-and-Maintenance-Operations) The outside contractor has the responsibility for safely maintaining work sites.

## Connection Fees

View the [Water/Sewer Fees and Rates (PDF)](https://www.livingstonnj.org/DocumentCenter/View/8868/Water-Sewer-Fee-List-PDF). Connection fees depend on the number of units or proposed usage at the structure and are calculated using an equivalent demand unit (EDU) of 300 gallons per day (GPD). The sizing of any new water or sewer service shall be determined using the building code plumbing calculations, but these numbers shall not be used for determining connection fees. New single family homes are always considered to be 1 EDU for connection fee purposes. For multi-unit residential properties, each individual dwelling shall be considered to be 1 EDU for connection fee purposes. For large commercial developments, the proposed usage must be determined using the NJDEP demand numbers listed in the demand numbers from NJAC 7:10 and NJAC 7:14A. Large scale commercial/residential developments are eligible to receive a prior use credit based on the previous three years of utility billing history at the property.

## New Meter Purchases & Construction Water

The new meter pricing can be found in the [Water/Sewer Fees and Rates (PDF)](https://www.livingstonnj.org/DocumentCenter/View/8868/Water-Sewer-Fee-List-PDF). Meters can be requested with [this form](/FormCenter/Water-Department-18/Demo-Preparation-Form-95). Once all the new utility connections have been completed and inspected satisfactorily and any outstanding fees have been fully paid, a new meter can be purchased. All meters must be purchased through the Township. The meter must match the size of the incoming line for all domestic and commercial meters. Lawn meters, where planned to be used, are recommended to match the size of the domestic or commercial meter. Fire meters must be installed on the backflow preventer assembly on the fire line. Typically, small meters are used for single family homes, lawn meters, fire meters, and small commercial businesses. Large commercial businesses and multifamily developments typically require large meters. The installation requirements for small and large meters can be found below:

* [Installation Requirements for Small Meters (2-inch and less) (PDF)](/DocumentCenter/View/30687/Livingston-Water---Small-Water-Meter-Instructions)
* [Installation Requirements for Large Meters (3-inch and greater) (PDF)](/DocumentCenter/View/30686/Livingston-Water---Large-Water-Meter-Instructions)

More information about construction water can be found on the [Hydrant Permits and Flow Tests page](https://www.livingstonnj.org/1524/Hydrant-Permits-and-Flow-Tests).
