# Hydrant Permits and Flow Tests

Hydrants are typically used by outside parties for temporary/construction water or to perform fire flow tests.

Per ordinance §316-14, it shall be unlawful for any person to furnish water to others for any purpose, open any fire hydrants of the Township or turn on water unless a special permit is obtained from the Division of Water and Sewers. Persons seeking to open a fire hydrant for any purpose shall be required to obtain, in addition to the special permit, a hydrant meter from the Township of Livingston Division of Water and Sewers.

## Hydrant Permits

All requests to use a fire hydrant must be submitted in writing to the [water@livingstonnj.org](mailto:water@livingsotnnj.org). For long-term commercial construction projects, the permit will be issued via the construction water. For long-term single family home construction projects, a water stub or temporary yard hydrant can be used in lieu of a fire hydrant. For temporary water supply for an event or short-term construction such as paving, a deposit will be required and a hydrant meter will be supplied.

### Construction Water

* Construction water fees are based on the total estimated cost of construction. The fees can only be calculated once building/demolition permits have been issued.
* Permission to use the hydrant will only be granted after fees have been paid. Only a single designated hydrant can be used.
* Depending on the timing of the permits and the construction process, construction water fees may be assessed twice. Once for the demolition permit and once for the building permit.
* A hydrant cannot be used without a backflow preventer. The site contractor must provide and install an RPZ or DCVA backflow preventer. This backflow preventer will remain on the single designated hydrant for the duration of the project and cannot be relocated without permission.
* For periods of extended hydrant use, the backflow preventer must be tested quarterly by a certified backflow prevention device tester with a valid backflow prevention device tester certificate. The backflow preventer must be taken out of service and repaired if it fails the quarterly test.
* Copies of the backflow preventer testing shall be submitted to [water@livingstonnj.org](mailto:water@livingsotnnj.org) by the end of the quarter on March 31, June 30, September 30, and December 31.
* Only proper hydrant wrenches will be permitted to be used on the hydrant. NO PIPE WRENCHES! Improper hydrant use will result in the permission to use the hydrant being revoked. Any damages to the hydrant will be charged to the property owner and site contractor.

### Temporary Water

* A surety deposit in the amount of $3,000 will need to be submitted to guarantee that no damage to the meter or hydrant will occur.
* A hydrant meter will be issued to a single designated hydrant. The hydrant meter cannot be relocated, and no alternate hydrants can be used.
* Only proper hydrant wrenches will be permitted to be used on the hydrant. NO PIPE WRENCHES! Improper hydrant use will result in the permission to use the hydrant being revoked and forfeiture of the security deposit. Any damages to the hydrant will result in forfeiture of the security deposit.
* Surety deposits will be returned following satisfactory inspection of the hydrant and meter for damage by Township personnel and payment of all outstanding meter rental and water charges by the applicant.
* The water bill will include the meter billed at a rate of $15.00 per day, until returned to the Township, plus metered water charges.

## Hydrant Flow Test

Requests to perform a flow test must be submitted in writing to [water@livingstonnj.org](mailto:water@livingsotnnj.org). The Township only witnesses the flow test and supports in the operation of the fire hydrants. They do not provide any equipment or perform the test. There is a $200 fee that can be paid on the day of the test with a check made out to the Township of Livingston. We also request a copy of the flow test results be submitted whenever they are available to [water@livingstonnj.org](mailto:water@livingsotnnj.org). Flow tests can only be performed on Wednesdays at 10:00 a.m., subject to the availability of Township Staff.
