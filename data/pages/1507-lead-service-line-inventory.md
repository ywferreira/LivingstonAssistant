# Lead Service-Line Inventory

## Unknown Water Service Line Material — Update 9/10/2025

You may have recently received the letter below notifying you that your property has “Unknown Service Line Material.” Receiving this letter does not mean you have a confirmed lead water service line, that lead has been detected in the water, that there has been a violation, or that you are required to do water samples to test for lead. It simply means that your water service line material is listed as "unknown" in the Township’s water service line inventory and until the water service material line is "known" it has a possibility of being lead. A water service line which has been partially identified is still considered unknown in the inventory, so you may be receiving this letter even though you may have already completed the “Report My Service Line Survey”.

This letter is a requirement introduced last year by the Lead and Copper Rule Revisions on top of the existing New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection (NJDEP) and Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) regulations. The requirement states that letters must be distributed to all properties listed as having unknown water service line material in the utility’s water service line inventory annually. This letter was previously distributed in November 2024. Property owners can help the Township complete its water service line inventory by completing the “Report My Service Line” survey. The survey and the Township of Livingston’s water service line inventory is available at the top of this page.

## Why are we doing this?

The New Jersey State Legislature recently passed P.L.2021, Ch. 183, a law enforced by the New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection (NJDEP) which requires that all lead and galvanized waterlines be inventoried and replaced over the next ten (10) years. This is a preventative program to remove lines that can — under some circumstances — lead to elevated lead levels in water. This does not mean that your property has lead action-level exceedance.

The overwhelming majority of the service lines within the Township are comprised of copper and/or plastic; galvanized services are occasionally found — especially on the customer side of the service. While galvanized services are not comprised of lead, the NJDEP considers them equivalent to lead for tracking purposes. Lead services are very rarely found and when they are, efforts are made to replace them as soon as possible. Please note a portion of the service line is owned by the customer and a portion of the service line is owned by the utility. If the customer side is found to be lead or galvanized, they will be responsible for replacing that portion of the line. Please go to the [Utility Service-Line Ownership](/1568/Utility-Service-Line-Ownership) page for more information about water service line ownership.

## Lead Service Line Replacement Program

For any service positively identified as lead, the service must be replaced. For the property owner's side, it is the property owner's responsibility to replace the line, but the Township will coordinate with the property owner including requesting a waiver for any plumbing permit fees with the Building Department, locating the outside water shutoff, confirming the water service material at the shutoff, replacing the utility side of the service if it is identified to be lead, and waiving the shutoff fees for replacement. \*Note that per C.58:12A-41, section 2, the definition of a lead service line now includes lead connectors (lead gooseneck, lead pigtail, or other lead fitting) and galvanized service lines. Galvanized service lines are typically found on the property owner's side of the water service and prone to failure due to age. Partial replacement of any water service line is not permitted.

If you are planning to replace your water service line, regardless of whether it is lead or non-lead, please contact us at [water@livingstonnj.org](mailto:water@livingstonnj.org) or 973-535-7951 prior to replacement so that we can coordinate our efforts.

## Lead Water Testing

At this time, lead water test kits are only available for properties where there is a confirmed lead line as identified through the inventory or a lead service line was recently replaced at the property. Post lead service line removal water testing services are available upon request 3 to 6 months after the lead service replacement. For those who would like to voluntarily pursue water testing, view the [list of NJDEP Certified Drinking Water Laboratories (PDF)](https://www.livingstonnj.org/DocumentCenter/View/27866/Drinking_Water_Certified_Lead_Labs) capable of performing lead water tests.
