# Construction Debris

Any materials that are the result of demolition, alterations, or repairs to buildings, homes, or other structures are not part of regular or bulk garbage collection.

Homeowners or their contractors must, by law, arrange for removal of any of these materials.

Construction debris is**not** picked up by the Township’s regular garbage collection vendor on regular *or* bulk collection days.

The following materials will **not** be collected by the Township’s solid waste collector:

* Ashes
* Automobile parts (other than tires)
* Bricks
* Combustible, explosive, or caustic materials
* Concrete
* Designated recyclable material per Township Ordinance 40-2008 and 2006 Solid Waste Management Plan per [Essex County Utilities Authority website](http://ecuanj.com/mandatory-recyclable-materials.html).
* Electronics (including, but not limited to computer monitors, televisions, computer CPUs, copiers, fax machines, laptops, cell phones, telephones, stereos, PDAs)
* Household Hazardous Waste (including, but not limited to paint, paint thinners, solvents, gasoline, car batteries, household cleaning fluids, etc.)
* Materials resulting from demolition, alternations, or repairs to buildings or other structures
* Other appliances/Scrap Metal(including, but not limited to refrigerators, freezers, air conditioners, washers, dryers, desks, auto parts)
* Rocks
* Sod
* Soil
* Shipping crates
* Stones
* Stumps
* Trees (other than branches in bundles)
* Wood pallets

[View Ordinance Chapter 232. Recycling: Subsection 242-4 Disposition of recyclable materials (I)](https://www.ecode360.com/10300564?highlight=recyclable,recycling&searchId=15934338057789951#10300564)

## Arranging for Disposal of Construction-Related Trash/Debris

In order to dispose of construction or demolition materials or other residential bulky waste that cannot be picked up, residents may choose to:

* Have their contractor or an appropriately licensed hauler properly dispose of the debris,
* Secure a dumpster themselves, or
* Bring the materials to Essex County’s contracted waste disposal site (call for drop-off hours):
  + Interstate Waste Services -   
    DART Transfer Station, 540 Doremus Ave., Newark, NJ 201-830-3250 ext. 3437

Residents are responsible for arranging for the proper disposal of all such materials.
